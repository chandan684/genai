📚 KNOWLEDGE BASE

A heat engine converts thermal energy into mechanical work by exploiting temperature differences between a hot source and cold sink. According to the Second Law of Thermodynamics, no engine can be 100% efficient. The Carnot engine represents the theoretical maximum efficiency for any heat engine operating between two temperatures — a benchmark all real engines strive toward but never reach.
